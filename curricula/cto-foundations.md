# CTO Foundations

**Slug:** `cto-foundations`  
**Duration:** 4–6 weeks · 2 sessions/week  
**Level:** First-time CTO / new-to-seat · also refresh for seasoned

## Outcomes

- Clear written role charter (own / share / escalate)
- Calendar that protects leverage (maker vs manager vs exec time)
- Decision log habit with reversible vs irreversible framing
- Personal operating cadence (1:1s, staff, board prep)

## Modules

### M1 — Role clarity
- Objectives: Define CTO job for *this* company stage
- Drill: Write a one-page charter; professor stress-tests it
- Sources: High Output Management (Grove); The Manager's Path (Fournier) — chapters on managing managers

### M2 — Leverage & calendar
- Objectives: Kill low-leverage work; protect deep work and people time
- Drill: Redesign next week’s calendar against leverage rules
- Sources: Grove on leverage; public essays on maker’s schedule (Graham)

### M3 — Decision hygiene
- Objectives: Classify decisions; document; avoid consensus theater
- Drill: Take 3 live decisions → log with options/tradeoffs/owner
- Sources: Bezos-style one-way/two-way door framing (public shareholder letters)

### M4 — Operating cadence
- Objectives: Design staff meeting, 1:1 system, escalation paths
- Drill: Propose a 30-day cadence; critique failure modes
- Exit rubric: Charter + cadence doc + 5 decision-log entries reviewed

## Assessment

| Criterion | Pass |
|-----------|------|
| Charter specificity | Stage-specific, not generic |
| Calendar leverage | ≥1 protected deep block/week shown |
| Decision log | 5 real entries with tradeoffs |
